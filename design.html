My process

My process tends to be very informal but comprises of an iterative approach using varied methods as needed. It may start out as simple as writing a doc with basic requirements up until implementing designs for production and validating them. In between I make use of different techniques to map out the user flows, explore and refine solutions using low to high fidelity mock-ups, plan and participate on user research, implement, run AB tests and analyse their results.

Booking.com

Pay at the Property (2020)

A new end-to-end approach allowing guests to pay for their reservations at the property whilst giving accommodations more protection.

What I've done
  • Partner (accommodations) journey flows for both opting into the new product and managing reservations within that new flow.
  • Low and high fidelity mock-ups on different stages incorporating feedback from key stakeholders across the business.
  • Collaborated with User Researcher in planning research initiatives on this topic, discussing questions we needed answer for and writing script for interviews.
  • Conducted multiple partner interviews and debriefed them with the team discussing key insights to plan further steps.

Transactional Policies (2019 – 2021)

A complete overhaul on the company's cancellation policies for both guests and accommodations.

What I've done
  • Low fidelity visualisation of a high complexity and high uncertainty project for both the policies simplication set-up for accommodations as well full flows on all main policies + payment timing combination scenarios.
  • Sought out alignment with multiple parties and stakeholders across the business and iterated on those mock-ups whilst raising visibility of the project across the organisation.
  • Flagged potential problems and issues early on with our org's leadership so we could adapt quickly and discuss with relevant stakeholders a plan of action.

Partner performance tools: Opportunity Center and Analytics (2014 – 2016)

A business recommendations hub and a analytics reporting dashboard for accommodations.

What I've done
  • Gathered requirements and worked close with the PM to refine the problem statement and the projects' value propositions through an iterative process using low fidelity mock-ups.
  • Participated in multiple user research debrief sessions in which we discussed key findings and next steps, problems we should focus on and how iterated on the product.
  • As we moved on I started refining those mock-ups using more high-fidelity language to get closer to what would be the real thing and gather further feedback from our potential users.
  • Implemented the whole product's design for production in collaboration with my team's front-end developer using Bootstrap, the framework in which the Extranet was built upon.
  • Kept iterating on the MVP using insights from further research round and partners using it as part of a beta program.
  • Launched the product and kept learning from users behavior and optimising it using AB experimentation.

Petrobras

The biggest brazilian company operating in the oil industry. I used to work in its R&D IT department in which I designed several scientific applications of high complexity to be used for refinery work.

What I've done

  • Loads of low to high fidelity wireframes and prototypes to propose solutions for highly complex problems and user interactions to make it easier for scientists and engineerings using digital systems.
  • Conducting end user interviews and having a close collaboration with them to refine the understanding of their problems and refining the solutions together.
  • Visual design and markup implementation mostly using Bootstrap.
